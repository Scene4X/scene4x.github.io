Call for Papers Challenge Tracks

Introduction

Spatial world understanding is fundamental to autonomous intelligence. Recent advances in 3D/4D scene reconstruction, generative models, foundation models, and world models have enabled increasingly realistic environment modeling, yet most approaches remain disconnected from downstream autonomy, limiting their impact on decision-making, simulation, and real-world deployment.

Scene4X bridges scene learning and autonomous intelligence by unifying spatial representation learning, generative world models, and embodied reasoning. The workshop focuses on geometry-aware, dynamic, and uncertainty-aware spatial worlds that directly support downstream tasks in autonomous driving, robotics, and aerial systems.

We invite contributions on 3D/4D reconstruction, generative neural representations, multimodal spatial foundation models, world models for closed-loop simulation, and applications to planning and safety evaluation. Scene4X will feature invited talks, paper presentations, a panel discussion, and two challenge tracks (OccuFly and World Model Scene Generation Benchmark) to promote reproducible evaluation.

By connecting scene modeling with action and autonomy, Scene4X advances a new paradigm: learning scenes not only to render them, but to reason, simulate, and act within them.

Topic of Interest

Spatial Representation & Reconstruction

  • 3D/4D reconstruction and dynamic scene understanding
  • Neural fields, Gaussian representations, and geometry-aware learning
  • Cross-view consistency, temporal coherence, and uncertainty estimation

Generative World Models

  • Controllable scene generation and video world simulation
  • Physics-aware and interaction-aware generation
  • Closed-loop world models for policy development and testing

Foundation Models for Spatial Intelligence

  • Multimodal pretraining for spatial reasoning
  • Language-grounded scene understanding and embodied QA
  • Generalization across domains, sensors, and environments

Autonomy Applications

  • Autonomous driving, robotics, and aerial systems
  • Planning, risk assessment, and safety evaluation
  • Sim2real transfer and reproducible benchmarks

Paper Track

  • Archival and non-archival workshop submissions are both welcome.
  • Submission topics cover all Scene4X core themes and interdisciplinary applications.
  • Double-blind reviewing with emphasis on novelty, technical quality, and reproducibility.
  • Accepted papers will be presented as oral spotlight or poster presentations.

Challenge Tracks

OccuFly Open challenge: Semantic Scene Completion

This track focuses on semantic scene completion: reconstructing complete 3D semantic scenes from partial observations with strong geometric and semantic consistency. More details on OccuFly.

World Model Scene Generation Benchmark

This benchmark evaluates whether generated critical scenes are reasonable for downstream autonomous driving and robotics tasks.
